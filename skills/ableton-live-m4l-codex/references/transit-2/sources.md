# Transit 2 Sources

Date captured: 2026-05-30

## Primary sources

### Baby Audio product page

URL: https://babyaud.io/transit

Why it matters:

- Official positioning: Transit 2 is a Baby Audio and Andrew Huang
  collaboration for transitions, rhythmic movement, sidechain motion, and more.
- Lists six motion modes: Macro, LFO, Follower, Sidechain, Gate, Sequencer.
- Lists 28 effect modules and current platform/format support.
- States Ableton Live support among major DAWs.
- Includes changelog notes such as v2.2 adding an anniversary preset pack on
  2025-11-13.

Skill takeaways:

- Macro mode maps directly to the current Ableton-exposed `1. MACRO CONTROL`.
- Sidechain/Follower modes are relevant future references for drum-reactive
  motion.
- The official "Smoothing" and "FX on at 0%" settings matter when trying to
  avoid abrupt transitions.

### Transit 2 manual

URL: https://www.lootaudio.com/_media/images/loot/Baby%20Audio/transit-2/Transit%2B2%2BBaby%2BAudio%2BManual.pdf

Why it matters:

- Describes preset browser behavior and effect-module workflow.
- Notes effect-chain reordering, module presets, randomization, and global mix
  / output controls.
- Confirms common plugin formats and shortcut behavior.

Skill takeaways:

- Preset and module-level workflows are important when the agent asks the
  producer to audition Transit 2.
- Module ordering is part of sound design evidence, not incidental UI.

### Baby Audio interview with Andrew Huang

URL: https://babyaud.io/blog/andrew-huang-interview

Why it matters:

- Explains the creative intent behind Transit 2.
- Andrew explicitly frames the macro knob as a "sweet spot finder," which
  supports this skill's safe-point-first practice.
- Highlights Follower and Sidechain modes as ways for effects to derive motion
  from audio.
- Mentions DAW automation as a way to go beyond the macro alone.

Skill takeaways:

- Safe macro points are not a workaround; they match the product's own creative
  affordance.
- The next research branch should compare Macro, Sidechain, and Follower on the
  same preset.

## Secondary sources

### MusicRadar launch article

URL: https://www.musicradar.com/music-tech/my-new-plugin-does-ridiculous-things-baby-audio-and-andrew-huang-release-transit-2-the-latest-version-of-their-multi-effects-plugin-that-kills-static-mixes

Why it matters:

- Good high-level framing: transition workstation plus motion for static parts.
- Summarizes new motion modes, 28 effects, and examples like risers,
  tape-stop/rewind style effects, Looper, Warp, Reverser, chorus, EQ, and
  compressor.

Skill takeaways:

- Do not limit Transit 2 to dramatic transitions; it can also add restrained
  movement to pads, guitars, piano, and other static layers.

### MusicTech Transit 2 review

URL: https://musictech.com/reviews/plug-ins/baby-audio-transit-2-review-andrew-huang/

Why it matters:

- Review context for usability and risk of overusing a fast transition tool.
- Useful as a reminder that ease of use can become a bias toward too much
  movement.

Skill takeaways:

- Treat Transit 2 as a Capability-Bias Pair: it enables fast movement, but may
  tempt the agent to over-animate.

### Original MusicTech Transit review

URL: https://musictech.com/reviews/plug-ins/baby-audio-transit-review/

Why it matters:

- Background on the original Transit concept and transition-designer workflow.
- Useful for understanding why Transit 2 still retains the macro-transition
  identity even as it expands into broader motion effects.

Skill takeaways:

- Preserve the distinction between "transition designer" and "constant
  movement effect."
